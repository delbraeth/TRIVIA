🧠 General Trivia

History Cinco de Mayo ≠ Mexican Independence Day. It commemorates the Battle of Puebla (May 5, 1862) where Mexican forces defeated a French invasion. Mexican Independence Day is September 16 — and Cinco de Mayo is a much bigger deal in the U.S. than in most of Mexico.
Animals A group of sharks is called a "shiver."
Food Carrots were originally purple — Dutch farmers selectively cultivated orange varieties in the 17th century, possibly to honor the Royal House of Orange.
Language The word "salary" comes from Latin salarium — Roman soldiers were given an allowance to purchase salt (sal). That's also where "not worth his salt" comes from.
Engineering The first Ferris wheel was built for the 1893 Chicago World's Fair by engineer George Washington Gale Ferris Jr. — designed as America's answer to the Eiffel Tower.
Animals Crows can recognize individual human faces, remember people who wronged them, and teach younger crows to distrust those same faces — sometimes for years.
Geography The Amazon River discharges more freshwater into the ocean than the next seven largest rivers combined — roughly 20% of all river water flowing into the world's oceans.
Science Lightning strikes Earth approximately 100 times per second — about 8.6 million times a day.
Animals A flamingo can only eat with its head upside down — its uniquely curved bill filters food while inverted.
Astronomy There are more stars in the observable universe than grains of sand on all of Earth's beaches combined (roughly 10²⁴ vs. 10¹⁹).
