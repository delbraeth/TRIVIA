General Trivia — Did You Know?

Science
A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus — it rotates so slowly that its day (243 Earth days) exceeds its year (225 Earth days).
Food
Cashews are always sold shelled because their shells contain urushiol — the same toxic chemical found in poison ivy.
Geography
Canada has more lakes than the rest of the world combined — over 3 million of them, holding about 20% of the world's fresh water.
Sports
The Boston Marathon was first run in 1897 — making it the world's oldest annual marathon. It's held every Patriots' Day (3rd Monday of April).
History
Cleopatra lived closer in time to the Moon landing (1969) than to the construction of the Great Pyramid (~2560 BC). She was born ~69 BC.
Pop Culture
The TV show Seinfeld was originally pitched as a show about "nothing" — but the actual NBC pitch title was "The Seinfeld Chronicles."
Animals
A group of flamingos is called a flamboyance. Their pink color comes entirely from the carotenoid pigments in the shrimp and algae they eat.
Oddity
Oxford University is older than the Aztec Empire. Teaching began there around 1096–1167 AD; the Aztec Empire was founded in 1428.
Language
The word "quiz" has no clear etymology — it appeared suddenly in Dublin around 1791. Some say a theater owner bet he could introduce a new nonsense word to the language overnight.
Music
"Happy Birthday to You" was the first song performed in outer space — sung by the Gemini 9 astronauts in 1966 for fellow astronaut Gene Cernan's birthday.
Drink
Champagne must come from the Champagne region of France; otherwise it's called crémant, prosecco, or cava depending on where it's made.
Anatomy
The human nose can detect over 1 trillion distinct scents — far more than the ~10 million colors and ~340,000 tones the eye and ear can distinguish, respectively.
