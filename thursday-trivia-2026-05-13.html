🧠 General Trivia & "Did You Know"
Animals
Octopuses have three hearts and blue, copper-based blood (hemocyanin instead of hemoglobin). Two hearts pump blood through the gills; the third pumps it to the rest of the body — and shuts off when the octopus swims.
Animals
Wombat droppings are cube-shaped — the only known cube-shaped feces in nature. Their colons form the shape so the scat stays in place when used to mark territory.
Food
Botanically, bananas are berries — and strawberries are not. (A berry, by botanical definition, must have seeds inside the fleshy pulp from a single ovary.)
Food
Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found 3,000-year-old honey in Egyptian tombs that was still edible — its low water content and acidity make it nearly immortal.
Geography
The world's shortest scheduled commercial flight runs in Scotland between Westray and Papa Westray (Orkney Islands) — about 90 seconds gate-to-gate, sometimes 47 seconds with a tailwind.
Statue of Liberty
Lady Liberty's nose is 4 feet 6 inches long; her index finger is 8 feet long; and her sandal is a size 879. The whole statue is 305 feet tall to the tip of the torch.
Language
The dot above the lowercase letters "i" and "j" is called a tittle — from Latin titulus, the same root as "title."
Science
Sharks predate trees. Sharks have existed for ~400 million years; the first trees evolved roughly 350 million years ago. Sharks also predate Saturn's rings.
Time
A "jiffy" is a real unit of time — in physics, about 1/100 of a second (it's also been defined as the time it takes light to travel 1 cm in a vacuum).
Geography
The Great Wall of China is NOT visible from space with the naked eye — a popular myth dispelled by China's own astronaut Yang Liwei after he returned from orbit in 2003.
Customs
In Sweden, Saturday is officially "Lördagsgodis" — Saturday candy day — when children are encouraged to eat sweets, a custom traced back to a state-sponsored dental health campaign in the 1950s.
