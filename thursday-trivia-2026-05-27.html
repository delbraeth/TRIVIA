This Day in History — May 27

1703
Czar Peter the Great founded the city of St. Petersburg on the Neva River delta, building it largely on drained marshland. He made it Russia's capital in 1712; it was the empire's capital until the 1917 Revolution. Over 100,000 serfs died building it.
1930
The Chrysler Building opened in New York City, becoming the world's tallest structure at 1,046 feet — briefly. Just 11 months later, the Empire State Building topped it. The art deco eagle gargoyles on its 61st floor are iconic.
1937
"Pedestrian Day" — Golden Gate Bridge opened to foot traffic in San Francisco. About 200,000 people walked across on opening day. Cars were allowed the next day (May 28). The bridge was built in 4 years for ~$35M.

Founded & Invented

What year was Home Depot founded, and by whom?
1978 — Bernie Marcus & Arthur Blank, Atlanta, GA
Both were fired from Handy Dan hardware stores; co-founded the competitor that became the world's largest home improvement chain
Blockbuster Video — when founded, and what happened to it?
1985 — David Cook, Dallas, TX
At peak: 9,000+ stores, 84,000 employees. Filed for bankruptcy 2010. One store remains (Bend, Oregon)
Who invented cotton candy, and what was their day job?
William Morrison (confectioner) & John Wharton — a dentist! (1897)
The electric spun-sugar machine debuted at the 1897 Tennessee Centennial Expo; sold 68,655 boxes at 25¢ each
ByteDance / TikTok — year founded and founder?
2012 — Zhang Yiming, Beijing
TikTok launched internationally in 2017; ByteDance is now one of the world's most valuable private companies
Zoom Video Communications — founded when and by whom?
2011 — Eric Yuan, San Jose, CA
Yuan had proposed the idea to Cisco (his employer) and was rejected; built Zoom himself. IPO in 2019; exploded during COVID-19
Who invented the Leatherman multi-tool, and how long did it take?
Tim Leatherman — started 1975, first sale 1983 (8 years!)
Got the idea from a trip to Europe when he needed a tool. Over 100 million Leathermans sold since

General Trivia

History
Shortest US presidency: William Henry Harrison — 31 days, 12 hours (1841). He gave the longest inauguration speech in US history (~2 hours) in cold rain without a coat or hat, developed pneumonia, and died. His VP John Tyler became so despised after vetoing party bills that both parties expelled him.
American History
The 50-star US flag was designed by a 17-year-old for a school project. Robert Heft of Lancaster, Ohio submitted it in 1958 — his teacher gave him a B-minus. The teacher promised to change the grade if Congress adopted the design. It was. Grade changed to an A.
Animal Groups
A group of peacocks is called a "muster" (also "ostentation" or "pride"). A group of narwhals is a "blessing."
Astronomy
Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a storm that has been raging for at least 350 years. At its largest historically, it was so wide that three Earths could fit inside it. It's been slowly shrinking since the 1800s.
Science / Nature
Tardigrades (water bears, ~0.5mm long) are nearly indestructible — they survive the vacuum of space, temperatures from -458°F to +300°F, and 1,000× the radiation dose lethal to humans. They've been intentionally launched into orbit and survived.
Food History
Nutella was born of WWII shortages. Italian chocolatier Pietro Ferrero mixed ground hazelnuts (grown locally in Piedmont) with scarce chocolate in 1946 to stretch the supply. Today, Nutella uses roughly 25% of the world's entire hazelnut harvest every year.
Language
The Welsh town Llanfairpwllgwyngyllgogerychwyrndrobwllllantysiliogogogoch (58 letters) translates to: "Saint Mary's Church in the Hollow of the White Hazel near the Rapid Whirlpool of Llantysilio of the Red Cave." It's on the island of Anglesey.
Inventions
The typewriter was invented 8 years before the telephone — Christopher Sholes patented it in 1868; Bell's telephone came in 1876. The first novel typed on a typewriter? Mark Twain submitted the manuscript of Tom Sawyer by typewriter in 1875.
Geography
Norway has the world's 2nd-longest coastline (after Canada) — roughly 100,000 km (62,000 miles) due to its thousands of fjords. That's enough to wrap around the Earth about 2.5 times.
Climate / History
The Sahara was green just 5,000–11,000 years ago. During the "African Humid Period" (Green Sahara), monsoon rains supported lakes, rivers, hippos, and human settlements across what is now desert. Climate shifts ended it around 3000 BC.
Pop Science
The human brain generates about 20 watts of electrical power when active — enough to dimly light a small bulb. Despite being only ~2% of body weight, it consumes about 20% of the body's total energy.
