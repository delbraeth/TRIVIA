🧠 General Trivia

Hummingbirds are the only birds that can fly backward.
Vatican City is the world's smallest country — about 0.49 km² (roughly 121 acres).
There are more trees on Earth (~3 trillion) than stars in the Milky Way (~100–400 billion).
The fear of long words is, ironically, called "hippopotomonstrosesquippedaliophobia."
The Statue of Liberty's official name is "Liberty Enlightening the World."
Almonds aren't true nuts — they're the seeds of a drupe (stone fruit), like peaches and cherries.
Elephants are the only mammal that can't jump.
The Moon drifts about 3.8 cm farther from Earth every year.
Babies are born with about 300 bones; adults have 206 — many fuse as we grow.
There's no sound in space: a vacuum has no air to carry sound waves.
The Sun is actually white — it only looks yellow because Earth's atmosphere scatters its light.
Like fingerprints, every person's tongue print is unique.
