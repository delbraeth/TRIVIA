🧠 General Trivia

  • Cleopatra was actually of Greek (Ptolemaic) descent, not Egyptian — and the first ruler in her dynasty to learn the Egyptian language.
  • Sudan has more pyramids than Egypt — roughly 200–250 vs. ~120 (built by the Kushite kings of Nubia).
  • ZIP in ZIP code stands for Zone Improvement Plan (introduced by USPS in 1963).
  • Mauna Kea is the tallest mountain on Earth measured base-to-peak — about 33,500 ft (most of it underwater).
  • Cashews grow on the outside of a fruit called the cashew apple — and the shell contains the same irritant as poison ivy.
  • Vincent van Gogh sold only one painting during his lifetime — The Red Vineyard.
  • A blue whale's heart weighs about 400 lbs — roughly the size of a small car. Its arteries are wide enough for a human to crawl through.
  • Snails have about 14,000 microscopic teeth on a tongue-like structure called a radula.
  • Olympic flame is traditionally lit using a parabolic mirror at ancient Olympia, Greece — even today.
  • Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system (~864 °F surface) — not Mercury — thanks to its dense CO₂ atmosphere.
  • The first heart transplant was performed by Dr. Christiaan Barnard in Cape Town, South Africa, on Dec 3, 1967.
  • Letter "Q" is the only letter that doesn't appear in any U.S. state name.
