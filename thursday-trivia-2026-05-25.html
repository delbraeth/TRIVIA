📅 This Day in History — May 25
1935
Babe Ruth hit his 714th — and final — career home run at Forbes Field in Pittsburgh. He retired just weeks later. That record stood for nearly 40 years.
1961
President JFK challenged Congress to put a man on the Moon "before this decade is out" — launching the Apollo program that succeeded with Apollo 11 in July 1969.
1977
Star Wars (Episode IV: A New Hope) opened in US theaters — today is its 49th anniversary! George Lucas's space opera became the highest-grossing film in history at the time.
1979
American Airlines Flight 191 crashed after takeoff at Chicago O'Hare, killing all 273 on board plus 2 on the ground — the deadliest US aviation disaster to that point.
2008
NASA's Phoenix lander touched down near Mars's north pole to search for evidence of water and conditions suitable for microbial life.

🧠 General Trivia
1
Wombats are the only animals known to produce cube-shaped droppings. The purpose? The cubic shape keeps the poop from rolling off rocks and logs when they use it to mark territory.
2
Fortune cookies were invented in the United States — most likely San Francisco — by Japanese-American immigrants in the late 1800s. They are virtually unknown in China itself.
3
Earth has roughly 3 trillion trees — more than the estimated 100–400 billion stars in the Milky Way Galaxy.
4
The Canary Islands were not named after the yellow bird. The islands got their name first — from the Latin Insula Canaria, meaning "Island of Dogs." The birds were later named after the islands.
5
The Eiffel Tower grows about 6 inches (15 cm) taller in summer than winter, due to thermal expansion of its iron structure.
6
Humans are the only mammals with a true chin — a protruding lower jaw bone. No other mammal, not even our closest relatives, has one. Its evolutionary purpose is still debated.
7
Charles Osborne of Iowa set the Guinness record for the longest hiccuping spell: he hiccuped continuously from 1922 to 1990 — a total of 68 years.
8
The first baseball game ever broadcast on US national television featured the Cincinnati Reds vs. the Brooklyn Dodgers at Ebbets Field on August 26, 1939, on NBC's experimental station W2XBS.
9
Flamingos are naturally white or gray. They get their pink-to-red coloration from carotenoid pigments in the algae, shrimp, and crustaceans they eat. Flamingos in captivity fade without the right diet.
10
Maine is the only US state with a one-syllable name. It also borders just one other US state: New Hampshire.
11
The number of possible ways to arrange a standard 52-card deck (52 factorial) is roughly 8×1067 — more than the number of atoms on Earth. Every time you shuffle a deck, you almost certainly create an order that has never existed before.
12
The UK recorded its hottest May day in 79 years this past Sunday: 32.3°C (90°F) at Kew Gardens, London — a reminder that May heat records in Britain are not just a 2020s trend.
