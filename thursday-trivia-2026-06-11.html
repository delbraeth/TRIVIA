🧠 General Trivia & Did-You-Know

  • Slugs have four noses.
  • "Dreamt" is the only common English word that ends in the letters "mt."
  • The Statue of Liberty wears a size 879 sandal.
  • The little blob of toothpaste that sits on top of a toothbrush has a name — a "nurdle."
  • A flock of starlings wheeling and swooping together in the sky is called a "murmuration."
  • Apple seeds contain amygdalin, which releases tiny amounts of cyanide when crushed — you'd have to eat a great many to be harmed.
  • Lobsters taste with their legs and "smell" with their antennae.
  • The immortal jellyfish (Turritopsis dohrnii) can revert to an earlier life stage, in theory restarting its life cycle indefinitely.
  • A "baker's dozen" is 13 — medieval bakers tossed in an extra loaf to avoid harsh penalties for selling underweight bread.
  • A sea cucumber can expel its internal organs to startle a predator, then regrow them.
  • Cats have a third eyelid, called the nictitating membrane, that sweeps across the eye.
  • The word "brunch" is a portmanteau that first appeared in an 1895 British magazine article.
