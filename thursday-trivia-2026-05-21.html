🧠 General Trivia

AnimalsBumblebees can recognize human faces and even teach each other novel tasks — like rolling a tiny ball — by watching other bees.
ScienceHelium was discovered in the Sun's spectrum during an 1868 solar eclipse — 27 years before it was found on Earth. Named for helios (Greek sun).
AstronomySaturn's north pole has a hexagonal storm ~20,000 miles (32,000 km) wide — wider than two Earths. First spotted by Voyager 1 in 1981.
HistoryCoca-Cola contained trace cocaine (~9 mg per glass) until 1903; the company switched to "spent" coca leaves with the cocaine removed.
AnimalsA group of butterflies is a "kaleidoscope" (or a "flutter"); a group of jellyfish is a "smack."
BotanyStrawberries average ~200 seeds on the outside — and aren't true berries. Bananas, watermelons, and tomatoes are.
GeographyLake Titicaca (Peru/Bolivia border) is the world's highest navigable lake at ~12,500 ft (3,812 m).
LanguageA "pangram" is a sentence using every letter of the alphabet. "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog" is 33 letters with no Q-V-K cluster.
EngineeringThe concrete in Hoover Dam (completed 1935) is still curing — engineers estimate it will continue to harden for another 100+ years.
HistoryVikings reached North America (Newfoundland, L'Anse aux Meadows) ~1000 AD — nearly 500 years before Columbus.
AstronomyMount Everest grows ~4 mm taller per year as the Indian Plate continues to push under the Eurasian Plate.
AnatomyYour index finger has more touch receptors than your middle finger — only your lips and tongue are more sensitive.
