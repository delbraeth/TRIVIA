🏭 Founded & Invented
What year was ESPN founded, and in what city?
1979 — founded in Bristol, CT by Bill Rasmussen. It launched as the first all-sports cable network.
Who invented windshield wipers, and when?
Mary Anderson, in 1903. She was inspired after noticing a New York trolley driver had to open his window in the rain. She received a patent but couldn't sell the idea; it became standard by 1916.
Who invented the shopping cart (1937), and where?
Sylvan Goldman — grocery store owner in Oklahoma City. He noticed customers stopped shopping when their hand baskets got too heavy. Initial reaction: shoppers refused to use them out of embarrassment.
What year was Subway (the sandwich chain) founded, and how old was its co-founder?
1965 — Fred DeLuca was only 17 years old when he opened the first location in Bridgeport, CT with a $1,000 loan from family friend Peter Buck.
Who founded Adidas, and what's the connection to Puma?
Adolf "Adi" Dassler founded Adidas in 1949 in Herzogenaurach, Germany. His brother Rudolf had previously split off to found Puma — the two brothers became bitter rivals in the same small town.
Who invented the safety pin, and in what year?
Walter Hunt, in 1849. He invented it in about three hours to pay off a $15 debt — then sold the patent rights for $400. The buyer made a fortune.

Etymology
The smell of rain on dry earth has a name: petrichor. Coined in 1964 by Australian scientists Isabel Joy Bear and Richard Thomas — from the Greek petra (stone) + ichor (the fluid said to flow through the veins of the gods).
