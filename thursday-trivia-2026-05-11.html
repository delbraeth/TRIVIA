📅 This Day in History — May 11
1981
Bob Marley dies at age 36 — The reggae legend died of melanoma at a Miami hospital. He'd refused amputation of a toe years earlier on religious grounds. His funeral in Jamaica was a state event; a million people lined the route.
1997
Deep Blue beats Kasparov — Garry Kasparov, the reigning world chess champion, resigned after just 19 moves in Game 6 against IBM's Deep Blue. The first time a computer ever beat a reigning world champion in a match — a watershed moment for AI.
1927
The Academy is founded — The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences was founded at a banquet at the Biltmore Hotel in Los Angeles. The first Oscars ceremony followed two years later (1929) — and lasted 15 minutes.
1858
Minnesota becomes the 32nd state — Admitted to the Union on May 11, 1858. Today is officially Minnesota Statehood Day.

🎨 Arts & Literature
Which French novelist wrote Madame Bovary (1857), often called the first truly modern novel?
Gustave Flaubert — the book was put on trial for obscenity the same year; Flaubert was acquitted, and the publicity made it a bestseller.
Which Soviet/Russian composer wrote the 1936 symphonic fairy tale Peter and the Wolf, in which each character is voiced by a different orchestral instrument?
Sergei Prokofiev
Which French painter scandalized the Paris Salon in 1863 with Le Déjeuner sur l'herbe (Luncheon on the Grass) and again in 1865 with Olympia?
Édouard Manet — a key figure in the transition from Realism to Impressionism.
Which Canadian author wrote the dystopian 1985 novel The Handmaid's Tale?
Margaret Atwood
Which French Romantic painter created Liberty Leading the People (1830) — the bare-breasted Marianne raising the tricolor over the barricades?
Eugène Delacroix
Which African-American author wrote the 1940 novel Native Son, about Bigger Thomas in Depression-era Chicago?
Richard Wright
🏭 Founded & Invented
In what year — and by whom — was Toyota founded?
1937, by Kiichiro Toyoda — spun out of his father's loom-making business in Japan. The "d" in the family name was changed to "t" because "Toyota" took 8 brushstrokes in Japanese (a lucky number).
Who actually founded Tesla, Inc., and what year?
2003 — by engineers Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning. Elon Musk joined as Series A chairman / lead investor in 2004; he's "co-founder" only by retroactive legal settlement.
What year was Spotify founded, by whom, and in what country?
2006 — by Daniel Ek and Martin Lorentzon in Stockholm, Sweden. It launched publicly in 2008.
Who founded Wendy's, in what year and what city?
Dave Thomas, 1969, in Columbus, Ohio. The chain is named after his daughter Melinda, nicknamed "Wendy."
What year was PayPal founded, and what was it originally called?
1998 — originally named Confinity. It merged in 2000 with Elon Musk's online bank X.com, then rebranded as PayPal in 2001.
In what year was Ferrari founded, and where?
1939 — in Maranello, Italy, by Enzo Ferrari (initially as Auto Avio Costruzioni; the first Ferrari-branded car came in 1947).

🧠 General Trivia — Did You Know?
Animals
Hummingbirds are the only birds capable of true backwards flight — and the only birds that can hover in place. Their wings beat 50–80 times per second.
Animals
Hippos secrete a reddish-pink "sweat" (it's not actually sweat — it's hipposudoric acid) that acts as both natural sunscreen and an antibiotic against skin infection.
Religion / Tech
The Vatican has the world's only ATM with instructions in Latin — installed inside the Vatican Bank. The screen prompt: "Inserito scidulam quaeso ut faciundam cognoscas rationem."
Engineering
The American side of Niagara Falls was "turned off" in 1969 — the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers built a coffer dam diverting all water to the Canadian Horseshoe Falls, so geologists could study erosion at the base.
Animals
The cheetah can hit ~70 mph (113 km/h) — fastest land animal — but only sustains it for a few hundred yards before its body overheats.
Art
The Mona Lisa has no eyebrows or eyelashes — likely the fashion of Florentine noblewomen in da Vinci's time, who plucked them. (Some scholars believe restoration cleaned them off.)
Geography
Iceland has no mosquitoes — one of just a handful of places on Earth with none. The leading theory: the country's freeze-thaw cycle is too erratic for the larvae to survive.
Pop culture
Buzz Aldrin's mother's maiden name was Marion Moon — fitting, given he became the second human to walk on the Moon.
Science
The world's quietest room is Microsoft's anechoic chamber in Redmond, WA: officially −20.6 dBA — quiet enough to hear your own heartbeat, joints, and blood flow. People can't stand more than 45 minutes inside.
Geography
Saudi Arabia imports camels from Australia. A feral population of ~1 million dromedaries roams the Outback, descended from 19th-century work animals; many are exported back to the Middle East.
Engineering
The Great Pyramid of Giza held the record for tallest man-made structure on Earth for ~3,800 years — only surpassed by Lincoln Cathedral in England around 1311.
Astronomy
Astronauts on the ISS see ~16 sunrises and sunsets every 24 hours — the station orbits Earth once every ~90 minutes at 17,500 mph.
