🧠 General Trivia

Biology Octopuses have three hearts and blue, copper-based blood (hemocyanin instead of hemoglobin). Two hearts pump blood to the gills; the main heart pumps to the body — and it stops beating when they swim.
Evolution Sharks predate trees. Sharks date back ~450M years; the earliest trees appeared ~385M years ago.
Astronomy A bolt of lightning is ~30,000 K — about 5× hotter than the surface of the Sun (~5,778 K).
Food Honey never spoils. Edible honey has been recovered from 3,000-year-old Egyptian tombs — low water content + acidic pH prevent microbial growth.
Botany Bananas are berries; strawberries technically aren't (they're "aggregate accessory fruits").
History Cleopatra (d. 30 BC) lived closer in time to the building of the first Pizza Hut (1958) than to the building of the Great Pyramid of Giza (c. 2560 BC).
Language The Hawaiian alphabet has 13 letters: A, E, I, O, U + H, K, L, M, N, P, W, and the ʻokina (glottal stop).
Animals Wombat scat is cube-shaped — the only known cube-producing animal in the world. Helps them mark territory without it rolling away.
Astronomy Pluto, discovered in 1930, still hasn't completed one full orbit of the Sun — its year is 248 Earth years long.
Geography Antarctica is the largest desert on Earth (5.5M sq mi) — defined by precipitation, not heat; the interior averages <2 inches of snow a year.
Anatomy Babies are born with ~270 bones; adults have 206. Many fuse together during childhood (e.g., the skull plates).
Etymology Pencil "lead" is graphite — never lead. It was misidentified in the 1500s when a graphite deposit was found in Cumbria, England.
