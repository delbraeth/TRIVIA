🏭 Founded & Invented

Year & founders of Sears, Roebuck and Co.?
1893 — Richard Sears (watch salesman) + Alvah Roebuck (watchmaker), Chicago. Mail-order catalog reached 532 pages by 1897.
Year Thomas Edison patented his practical incandescent light bulb?
1879 (US Patent 223,898, Jan 27, 1880) — carbon-filament; 14.5 hours of light in first test, later improved to ~1,200 hours.
Inventor of the chocolate chip cookie & the inn it's named after?
Ruth Graves Wakefield, 1938 — at the Toll House Inn in Whitman, MA. Nestlé bought the rights and printed her recipe on the bag in exchange for a lifetime chocolate supply.
Year & humble origin of Marriott hotels?
1927 — J. Willard & Alice Marriott opened a 9-stool A&W root beer stand in Washington D.C. First hotel: Twin Bridges Motor Hotel (Arlington VA), 1957.
Year & team that isolated insulin for diabetes treatment?
1921 — Frederick Banting & Charles Best, University of Toronto. Banting + JJR Macleod shared the 1923 Nobel; Banting split his with Best.
Year Jonas Salk's polio vaccine was declared safe and effective?
April 12, 1955 — announced from University of Michigan, exactly 10 years after FDR's death (he had polio). Salk refused to patent it.

🧠 General Trivia

GeographyGreenland is the world's largest island (~836,330 sq mi). It is not a continent and is ~80% covered by ice sheet.
GeographyDead Sea shore is Earth's lowest dry land (~1,410 ft / 430 m below sea level) — shrinks ~3 ft per year.
BotanyHyperion, a coast redwood in California's Redwood National Park, is the tallest known tree on Earth (~380 ft / 116 m) — discovered 2006. Exact location kept secret to protect roots.
HistoryIceland's Althing (founded 930 AD at Þingvellir) is the world's oldest continuously functioning legislature.
AnimalsA group of crocodiles is called a "float" (in water) or a "bask" (on land). They can stay submerged 1+ hour, slowing heart rate to 2-3 bpm.
Law/FoodThe US Supreme Court ruled the tomato a vegetable for tariff purposes in Nix v. Hedden (1893). Botanically still a fruit.
AstronomyMercury, not Venus, is the planet that is on average closest to Earth — and on average closest to every other planet in the solar system (counter-intuitive but mathematically true).
TechThe first text message was sent Dec 3, 1992 — "Merry Christmas," from engineer Neil Papworth to Vodafone director Richard Jarvis.
AnimalsBald eagle nests can weigh 2+ tons; the largest recorded (in Florida) hit ~2.7 tons and 9 ft wide.
AnimalsThe wolverine is the largest land-dwelling member of the weasel family (Mustelidae) — not a bear.
Etymology"Banana republic" was coined by O. Henry in his 1904 short-story collection Cabbages and Kings, set in the fictional Honduras-inspired "Anchuria."
