🧠 General Trivia

  • Botanically, bananas are berries, but strawberries and raspberries are not — they're "aggregate fruits."
  • Mosquitoes kill more humans per year than any other animal on Earth — roughly 700,000 annually via the diseases they transmit.
  • The "black box" flight recorder on commercial airliners is actually painted bright orange — easier to spot in a crash.
  • A group of foxes is called a "skulk" (or a "leash").
  • The Sun contains 99.86% of all the mass in the solar system; Jupiter accounts for about 70% of what's left.
  • Pope John Paul I served only 33 days in 1978 before his sudden death — one of the shortest papacies in modern history.
  • The Bayeux Tapestry isn't actually a tapestry — it's embroidery, roughly 230 feet long, depicting the 1066 Norman conquest.
  • By royal decree, the Tower of London must keep at least six ravens — legend says the kingdom will fall if they leave.
  • Kongō Gumi, a Japanese temple-construction firm founded in 578 AD, is the world's oldest continuously operating company.
  • Papua New Guinea has over 800 living languages — more than any other country on Earth.
  • Eyeglasses (the wearable kind) were invented in Italy around 1290 — but no one is sure exactly who made the first pair.
  • The Komodo dragon's bite is venomous — not just bacteria-laden as long believed; venom glands were confirmed in 2009.
