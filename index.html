🧠 General Trivia

  • "Pencil lead" is actually graphite — never been lead. A 1564 storm in Borrowdale, England exposed the first deposit.
  • "OK" began as an 1839 Boston Morning Post joke for "oll korrect" — popularized by Martin Van Buren's "Old Kinderhook" campaign.
  • Antarctica is the only continent with no native reptiles, snakes, or amphibians.
  • Banana plants are technically herbs; the "trunk" is a pseudostem of tightly packed leaves.
  • The goldfish-5-second-memory thing is a myth — studies show they recall events for months.
  • Hot dogs were originally sold in the U.S. as "dachshund sausages" by German butchers in the 1860s.
  • The Hollywood Sign read "HOLLYWOODLAND" from 1923 to 1949 — a real-estate ad for a housing development.
  • The Atlantic Ocean is widening about 1 inch (2.5 cm) per year at the Mid-Atlantic Ridge.
  • Mozambique's flag is the only national flag to depict a modern firearm — an AK-47.
  • In colonial America, lobster was so plentiful it was fed to prisoners and indentured servants — laws limited servings to twice a week.
