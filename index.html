🧩 General Trivia
ANIMALS
Dolphins sleep with one eye open and half their brain active at a time — called unihemispheric sleep. The other half rests while they stay alert to predators.
SCIENCE
Photo 51 — the X-ray image of DNA taken by Rosalind Franklin in 1952 — provided Watson and Crick the key evidence to model the double helix. Franklin's contribution was long uncredited.
FOOD
Chocolate melts at ~93°F (34°C) — just barely below human body temperature (98.6°F). That's why it melts in your mouth but not easily in your hand.
LANGUAGE
"Serendipity" was coined in 1754 by Horace Walpole from the Persian fairy tale "The Three Princes of Serendip" — Serendip being the ancient Arabic name for Sri Lanka.
ANIMALS
A group of pigs is called a "sounder." Baby pigs are called piglets, and a female pig that hasn't had babies yet is a gilt.
HISTORY
The Roman Colosseum was completed in 80 AD under Emperor Titus and could hold 50,000–80,000 spectators. It had 80 arched entrances and a retractable awning (velarium).
SCIENCE/TECH
The first webcam (1991) was set up at Cambridge University's Computer Science Lab solely to watch a coffee pot — so researchers could check if it was empty before making the trip downstairs.
ANIMALS
A pistol shrimp (snapping shrimp) can snap its claw so fast it creates a cavitation bubble that briefly reaches ~8,000°F — hotter than the surface of the sun — and stuns or kills prey.
FOOD
The first breakfast cereal was "Granula," invented in 1863 by Dr. James Caleb Jackson in Dansville, NY — it was bran nuggets you had to soak overnight. Not a hit until Kellogg and Post came along.
BOTANY
A sunflower "head" is not a single flower — it contains up to 2,000 tiny individual flowers (florets). The outer petals are ray florets; the inner seeds are each a separate disc floret.
