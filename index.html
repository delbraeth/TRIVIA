📅 This Day in History — April 23

1564
William Shakespeare baptized (and believed born) in Stratford-upon-Avon, England. Remarkably, he also died on April 23, 1616 — making him the only major author commonly associated with the same birth and death date. He wrote 37 plays and 154 sonnets.
1791
James Buchanan born in Pennsylvania. He would become the 15th U.S. President (1857–1861) — the only president born in Pennsylvania, and the only one who never married, remaining a lifelong bachelor.
1871
The National Association of Professional Base Ball Players was founded — the direct forerunner to Major League Baseball and the first professional sports league in American history.
