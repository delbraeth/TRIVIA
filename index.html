🧩 General Trivia
🦐 Vision
A mantis shrimp has 16 types of color-detecting photoreceptors — humans have only 3.
🌡️ Speed of Sound
Sound travels ~4x faster through water (~1,484 m/s) than through air (~343 m/s).
🏔️ Alaska Purchase
The US bought Alaska from Russia for $7.2 million in 1867 ("Seward's Folly") — less than 2 cents per acre.
🌞 Slow Light
A photon takes ~100,000 years to travel from the sun's core to its surface — then only 8 minutes to reach Earth.
🦣 Elephants
Elephants (and hippos) are among the only large mammals that cannot jump. Their bones are too dense and they always have at least one foot on the ground.
🍦 Vanilla
Vanilla extract comes from the seed pods of the Vanilla planifolia orchid — originally cultivated in Mexico by the Totonac people.
🤖 Robot
The word "robot" was coined by Czech playwright Karel Čapek in his 1920 play R.U.R. (Rossum's Universal Robots).
🐛 First Computer Bug
The first computer "bug" was a real moth found stuck in a Harvard Mark II computer relay by Grace Hopper's team on Sep. 9, 1947.
🌊 Lake Superior
Lake Superior is the world's largest freshwater lake by surface area (~31,700 sq mi). Lake Baikal is deepest; Lake Michigan-Huron is larger by some measures.
📏 Napoleon's Height
Napoleon was NOT unusually short — he stood ~5'7", average for a Frenchman of his era. The myth came from British propaganda and a mix-up between French pouces and English inches.
