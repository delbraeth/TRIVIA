🧩 General Trivia
  • Biology
    Humans share approximately 50% of their DNA with bananas. We share ~98% with chimpanzees and ~85% with mice.
  • Food
    The green "wasabi" paste served at most American sushi restaurants is not real wasabi — it's horseradish dyed green. True wasabi is rare, expensive, and made from the Wasabia japonica plant.
  • Animals
    The platypus is one of only five species of egg-laying mammals (monotremes) — the others are four species of echidna. Male platypuses also have venomous ankle spurs.
  • Geography / Language
    Brazil is the only Portuguese-speaking country in South America. Despite being the largest country on the continent, all of its neighbors speak Spanish.
  • History / Food
    Cacao beans were used as currency by the Aztecs. According to 16th-century records, 100 cacao beans could buy a turkey — the same beans used to make chocolate.
  • Sports
    Table tennis was added as an Olympic sport at the 1988 Seoul Summer Games. China has won more Olympic table tennis gold medals than all other countries combined.
  • Etymology
    The word "disaster" comes from the Italian disastro — literally "bad star" (dis = bad + astro = star). Ancient astrologers blamed catastrophes on unfavorable celestial alignments.
  • Animals
    A group of cats is called a "clowder." A group of kittens is called a "kindle." A group of wild cats is called a "destruction."
  • Science
    The microwave oven was invented by accident in 1945 when Raytheon engineer Percy Spencer noticed radar waves had melted a chocolate bar in his pocket while he was working near a magnetron.
  • Food
    Peanuts are not actually nuts — they're legumes, in the same family as beans, lentils, and soybeans. True nuts (like walnuts and almonds) grow on trees; peanuts grow underground.
  • US Geography
    Maine is the only US state that borders exactly one other US state (New Hampshire). Every other state shares borders with at least two others.
  • Animals / Oceans
    Sea horses are the only animal in which the male becomes pregnant and gives birth. The female deposits eggs into the male's brood pouch, where he fertilizes and carries them for 2–4 weeks.
