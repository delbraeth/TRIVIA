🧠 General Trivia

Density Record: Monaco is the world's most densely populated country at ~19,000 people per square kilometer — more than twice as dense as Singapore.
Keyboard Trick: "Typewriter" can be spelled using only the top row of a QWERTY keyboard (Q-W-E-R-T-Y-U-I-O-P). Try it!
Fearless Badge: Honey badgers were named the world's most fearless animal by Guinness World Records. They've been observed fighting off lions, cobras, and porcupines — and can shrug off snake venom.
Tower Reprieve: The Eiffel Tower was originally scheduled for demolition in 1909, exactly 20 years after construction. It was spared because the antenna on top proved valuable for military radio transmissions — and Parisians had grown to love it.
Mpemba Effect: Under certain conditions, hot water can freeze faster than cold water. This phenomenon is named after Erasto Mpemba, a Tanzanian student who noticed it in 1963 while making ice cream.
Voice Acting Irony: Mel Blanc — the iconic voice of Bugs Bunny — was actually allergic to carrots. He had to spit them out after every recording take.
Hippo Group: A group of hippos is called a "bloat." Despite being mostly herbivores, hippos are considered one of Africa's most dangerous animals.
Playing Card Curiosity: The King of Hearts is the only playing card king depicted without a mustache — earning him the nickname "the Suicide King" (he appears to be thrusting a sword into his own head).
Pepper Science: The Scoville Scale, which measures chili pepper heat, was invented by pharmacist Wilbur Scoville in 1912. Pure capsaicin tops out at ~16 million Scoville Heat Units.
Ant Lifespans: A queen ant can live up to 30 years. Worker ants typically live only 1–3 years. The queen is the sole reproducing female in the entire colony.
